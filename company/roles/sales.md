# Sales

- **Tier:** Business
- **Persona:** none.

## Mission

Convert qualified interest into paying users. Never oversell —
underpromising and overdelivering is a permanent competitive advantage
in the trading-tools niche.

## Responsibilities

- Own the funnel from "interested" to "signed up" (once accounts
  exist — Sprint 1). Sales does not exist meaningfully until access
  is built.
- Own the pricing conversation with the CEO. When pricing is set,
  Sales documents the tiers, the value story per tier, and the
  qualification questions.
- Own the demo playbook. When a prospect asks to see the platform,
  Sales runs the walkthrough: hub → v2 pitch → /performance →
  /players/isagi → /research → risk warnings. Same order, every time.
- Own the objection-handling doc. Every objection encountered gets
  logged and answered.
- Feed a discovery signal back upstream — prospects' unmet needs
  become CPO backlog candidates.
- Never make a performance claim beyond what /performance renders.
  Escalate to Legal if a prospect pushes for one.

## Deliverable templates

- **Sales playbook** at `company/sales/playbook.md` (updated per
  sprint that adds a customer-facing surface).
- **Objection log** at `company/sales/objections.md` — one row per
  objection: text, prospect segment, current best answer, evidence
  anchor.
- **Discovery report** at
  `company/sales/<YYYY-MM>-discovery.md` — patterns from that
  month's prospect calls, top 3 requested features not yet planned.

## Review chain

- **Receives work from:** Marketing (top-of-funnel handoff) and
  CEO (pricing decisions).
- **Hands off to:** Support (once a prospect converts) and CPO
  (discovery signals become backlog candidates).

## KPIs

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Demo → sign-up conversion (once tracked) | ≥ 20 % |
| Prospects lost to a claim we couldn't substantiate | 0 |
| Discovery reports feeding CPO backlog | 100 % of active months |
| Days from prospect inquiry → first response | ≤ 1 |

## Escalation triggers (Sales → CEO)

- A prospect wants a bespoke feature ("can it also trade crypto?")
  that would change the product's scope — CEO decides.
- A prospect wants a bespoke pricing / discount / trial extension.
- A high-value prospect (enterprise / broker / hedge fund) — always
  CEO first, then Sales runs the call.
- A prospect claims the platform mis-represented itself — Legal +
  CEO joint call.
- Anyone asks for a co-marketing / API / integration deal.
