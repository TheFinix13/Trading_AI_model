\begin{abstract}
This report documents the design, statistical validation, and live deployment
of an automated foreign-exchange trading agent built around a single
counter-trend strategy: fading H4 supply/demand zone touches \emph{against}
the prevailing daily trend (\texttt{zone\_d1\_against}). Artificial
intelligence methods were applied not as a black-box predictor of price, but
as a rigorous research pipeline---hypothesis generation, ablation screening,
holdout testing, walk-forward validation, and cross-pair replication---to
separate genuine edge from overfitting. After six of seven initial ICT-style
concepts failed multiplicity-corrected significance tests, one cell survived
a full validation chain and was deployed on a three-pair demo portfolio with
strict risk constitution. Early live results (June 2026) are inconclusive
but consistent with backtested expectancy. The system is observation-heavy:
near-miss vaults, loss journals, and extension-target ladders collect data
for future studies without altering validated trade logic.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Retail and systematic traders frequently deploy complex multi-indicator
systems without pre-registered validation. The present work inverts that
pattern: \textbf{one strategy, one locked parameter set, one validation
chain}. The research question is operational, not philosophical:

\begin{quote}
\emph{Does fading H4 zone touches against the D1 trend produce positive
expectancy after transaction costs, across time and currency pairs, under
walk-forward and holdout discipline?}
\end{quote}

The agent implements this hypothesis mechanically. AI-assisted engineering
built the backtest harness, ablation grid, and live runner; human judgment
locked parameters and governs any future promotion.

\section{Related Work and Design Rationale}

The strategy belongs to the ICT (Inner Circle Trader) family of
supply/demand and higher-timeframe alignment concepts. Rather than
deploying the full ICT vocabulary, we tested seven concepts individually
(BOS, CHoCH, FVG, liquidity sweeps, order blocks, premium/discount, zone
alignment) and retained only what survived statistical gates. Counter-trend
zone fading against D1 bias emerged as the sole robust cell---with-trend
variants of the same logic failed, confirming that the edge is mean-reversion
specific, not generic ``zone trading.''

\section{Methodology}

\subsection{Strategy definition}
Locked parameters (frozen before out-of-sample tests):
\begin{itemize}
  \item Higher-timeframe alignment: D1, mode \texttt{against}
  \item HTF lookback: 10 bars; minimum D1 move: 60 pips
  \item Entry: H4 zone touch; fixed take-profit at 1.5R (never grid-swept)
  \item Soft stop at strategy level; broker catastrophe stop at 2.5$\times$ soft
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Validation pipeline}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Concept ablation} (7 ICT concepts): bootstrap $p$-values,
        Benjamini--Hochberg FDR at 5\%; six concepts rejected.
  \item \textbf{Holdout split}: in-sample 2015--2022, out-of-sample 2023--2025.
  \item \textbf{Walk-forward}: seven windows, 4-year IS / 1-year OOS;
        EURUSD H4/all: 7/7 positive OOS windows, median +11.34 pips/trade.
  \item \textbf{Cross-pair replication} (frozen parameters, scaled costs):
        GBPUSD +10.24 pips/trade ($p=0.001$, 11/11 years);
        USDCAD +4.63 pips/trade ($p=0.028$, 10/11 years);
        AUDUSD and NZDUSD excluded.
  \item \textbf{Sealed look} (2026 YTD): 16 trades, +7.75 pips/trade,
        $p=0.29$ --- inconclusive, monitoring continues.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Live architecture}
One process per symbol; deployment router maps
(symbol, timeframe, session) $\rightarrow$ risk scale. Deployed cells:
EURUSD H4 @ 1.0, GBPUSD H4 @ 0.5, USDCAD H4 @ 0.5. Adaptive position sizing
uses conviction band 0.5--2\% of balance $\times$ route scale. Unknown cells
fail-safe to skip.

\subsection{Live deployment (June 2026)}
Demo account (\$1000, Exness MT5) runs three symbol tabs. First confirmed
manual demo fill: EURUSD long, TP hit (+28.10 USD). Automated orders require
MT5 ``Algo Trading'' enabled; retcode 10027 rejections are logged when disabled.

\section{Limitations}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{target\_rr=1.5} was never grid-swept; all validation is
        conditional on this default.
  \item Sealed-period sample is small ($n=16$); no claim of live superiority
        yet.
  \item Single strategy family; portfolio diversification is across pairs,
        not alphas.
  \item Extension-target ladder and near-miss vaults are hypothesis-generating
        only---they do not alter live orders.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}

Applying AI to FX trading here meant building a \emph{validation apparatus},
not a neural network that ``learns'' from logs. One counter-trend zone cell
survived a pre-registered gauntlet and is deployed under strict risk limits.
The honest outcome is positive but modest: robust in backtest and walk-forward,
promising in early demo, not yet proven live at scale. Future work (parked
until trigger conditions): target\_rr study, partial take-profit policies,
USD-exposure manager, and autonomy-ladder research runners---all gated by
human approval and the same validation pipeline.
